Blog Post: The Art of the Follow-Up

Published on: July 18, 2025

In sales, the initial pitch is only the beginning. The real magic often happens in the follow-up. It's a delicate balance of persistence and respect, a dance of staying top-of-mind without becoming a nuisance. I've learned that a well-timed, value-added follow-up can be the single most important factor in closing a deal.

Instead of the generic "just checking in," I try to provide something of value. This could be a relevant industry article, an insight about a competitor, or a potential solution to a problem they mentioned in passing. It shows you were listening and that you're invested in their success, not just your commission.
